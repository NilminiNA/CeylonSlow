Beaches

5 beaches nobody else writes about

Not Unawatuna. Not Mirissa. Quiet, uncommercialised stretches of coast found by our community.

South Coast

Rekawa Beach

A turtle nesting beach south of Tangalle. No hotels, no touts — just a conservation project and one of the only places in Sri Lanka to see nesting leatherbacks at night. The community contacts at Rekawa Turtle Conservation Project will take you out after dark.

🐢 Turtle nesting Nov–Apr📍 Near Tangalle
East Coast

Okanda Beach

At the entrance to Kumana National Park, far east of Arugam Bay. Wild camping, almost no one else, and good snorkelling on the reef. 4WD or motorbike recommended to get here — makes it self-filtering.

🦎 Wild camping possible📍 Far East, Kumana
Northwest

Kalpitiya Lagoon

A vast shallow lagoon perfect for kite surfing and dolphin watching — the dolphin pods here number in the hundreds. Almost no infrastructure means almost no tourists. Stay in Kalpitiya town (basic guesthouses) and hire a boat from the fishermen.

🐬 Dolphins Nov–Apr📍 Kalpitiya Peninsula
North

Casuarina Beach, Karainagar

The far north has been largely off the tourist map since peace returned. Casuarina is a long, deserted beach backed by casuarina trees near Jaffna — calm, clean, and you may have it to yourself. Local guesthouses in Jaffna are welcoming and curious to host visitors.

🌊 Calm May–Oct📍 Near Jaffna
South Coast

Hiriketiya Back Bay

Not the main Hiriketiya bowl — walk around the rocks to the back bay. Half the people, calmer water, and local fishermen bring their boats in at sunrise. Best visited 06:00–09:00 before the day crowd arrives.

🌅 Best at sunrise📍 Hiriketiya, Dickwella
Villages & homestays

Rural Sri Lanka — the real thing

The Sri Lanka of small rice paddies, village tanks, and family homes that won't appear on Booking.com.

🌾 Knuckles Village Stays

Villages like Riverston and Pitawala Pathana in the Knuckles Range offer family homestays arranged through local guides. ~$15–25/night including meals. No electricity in some homes — that's part of it. Contact guides via our directory.

💡 Minimum 2 nights recommended — villagers invest time in getting to know you.
🏘 Nikaweratiya, North Central

A small market town in the dry zone near ancient reservoirs. Almost zero tourism infrastructure but warm hospitality. A community member has been connecting travellers with local families here — subscribe to our newsletter for the contact.

💡 Best during the dry season (Apr–Aug). Festival in May at the local temple is unmissable.
🏔 Ratnapura Gem District

Sri Lanka's gem mining heartland. Small villages where traditional pit mining still happens by hand. You can visit working mines with a local guide — not on any tour itinerary. The town of Ratnapura is the base; ask your guesthouse for a gem guide.

💡 Don't buy gems without a certified gemologist — the beautiful stones are real, the certificate might not be.
🌿 Uda Walawe Buffer Zone Villages

On the edge of Uda Walawe National Park, families offer accommodation that puts you among elephants without the overpriced safari lodge. Morning walks at dawn, elephants visible from the garden. Arrange through the park rangers or our directory.

💡 Elephants are most active at dusk and dawn — no safari vehicle required from these homestays.
Surf & ocean

Secret surf breaks

We're deliberately vague on exact locations to keep these uncrowded. Our directory has local surf guides who will take you there.

🏄 "The Bend" (South Coast)

A right-hand point break that works on the same swell as Hiriketiya but sees 1/10th of the crowd. 15-min motorbike from Dickwella. Works best at low-to-mid tide, 3–6 ft swell. Ask Kasun in our directory.

🏄 East Coast reefs (Arugam Bay area)

Everyone knows Main Point and Pottuvil Point. There are three other reef breaks in the area that the local surf community knows but don't advertise. Best Apr–Oct with the east swell. Local guide essential — some are shallow reef.

🏄 Northern reform breaks

The far north has virtually no surf culture yet — meaning no crowds. Works off the northeast monsoon (Dec–Mar). Long sandy beach breaks near Nilaveli that local fishermen know. Consistent but rarely above 4 ft.

🏄 Kalpitiya kite surfing

The lagoon's flat water and consistent wind (Dec–Mar, May–Oct) makes this world-class kite territory. One small local school — ask in Kalpitiya town. Rental boards available, instructor quality varies, bring your own bar if you can.

🤿 Pigeon Island snorkelling

Near Trincomalee — a small island with a marine national park. Hawksbill turtles almost guaranteed, blacktip reef sharks regular. Take the local fishermen's boats (far cheaper than official tours) from Nilaveli Beach.

🐋 Whale watching (Mirissa reality check)

Blue whales off Mirissa (Nov–Apr) are genuinely incredible and not as overhyped as the boat operators — but choose carefully. Small local operators (6–8 person boats) vs. large tourist boats: smaller is better for both experience and whale welfare.
